One Way — Point to point charter. The system automatically adds a positioning leg from your base to the client departure point, and a depositioning leg back to base after drop-off. All legs are billed at your hourly rate.
Return — Client departs and returns to the same location. If the aircraft waits, overnight and idle day rates apply. If the wait exceeds your threshold, the system automatically quotes a pick and drop instead — two separate one-way legs.
Safari / Multi-Leg — Multi-stop mission across multiple days. The aircraft follows the client between locations, accumulating overnight charges for every night out of base and idle day charges between legs. Used for safari circuits, expedition support, film shoots and multi-day campaigns.

Add Location

Your Locations Database Save any airstrip, helipad, private ranch, rooftop or landing zone you operate regularly. Once saved, your locations appear first in the autocomplete — before any Google Maps result — using the exact name you choose.

To add a location: Share a Google Maps pin or paste a Google Maps link into the search field — the system resolves the coordinates automatically. Then edit the display name to match how you refer to it operationally.

Aliases are alternative names or codes for the same location, separated by commas. Example: mara, keekorok, mm1 — any of these will match when quoting.

Examples: Miami Executive, USA  ·  Mara Keekorok, Kenya  ·  Biggin Hill, UK  ·  Maun Airport, Botswana  ·  Aspen Pitkin, USA

Your Aircraft Fleet Every aircraft you operate lives here. Add as many as you need — each one has its own hourly rate, cruise speed, base airport, overnight rate and mission costs. Active aircraft appear in all quotes simultaneously, allowing your clients to compare options side by side. Inactive aircraft are hidden from quotes but their configuration is preserved.

Enquiries — Your CRM Every quote generated by the system lands here automatically as a new enquiry. Track each lead through the full pipeline — from first contact through to invoice and payment. The pipeline stages are: Pending → Contacted → Accepted → Invoiced → Paid. Move enquiries between stages as the booking progresses.

From here you can build and send invoices, convert quotes to receipts, share documents via WhatsApp or email, and log payments against any document. Nothing gets lost and every interaction is on record.

Pro plan: Any client who downloads an auto-generated PDF from your client-facing quote page is automatically tracked here as a new enquiry — complete with their route, aircraft selection and contact details. Zero manual data entry.

Records & Document Manager Every document generated by QC Aero lives here — quotations, invoices and receipts. The system follows a structured flow: a quote becomes an invoice once confirmed, and an invoice becomes a receipt once paid. You can also create documents manually without running a quote first — useful for custom charters, retainers or non-standard billing.

Log: The log function on an invoice records a payment against that document and moves it to the receipt stage. Use it when a client pays outside the system — bank transfer, cash or mobile money.

All documents are stored securely on Google Firebase infrastructure with tenant-level data isolation. Your documents are never accessible to other operators on the platform.

Data security recommendations: Never share your admin password. Log out when using shared devices. Export important records periodically by downloading PDFs. Deletion of any record is permanent and irreversible — there is no recycle bin.
